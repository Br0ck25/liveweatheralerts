Nighttime Warning Checklist

Use this before bed so overnight warnings wake you quickly and your household can move safely.

Prep time: 5 minutes Goal: Fast action in the dark Best for: Storm season nights

Before Bed: 5-Minute Prep

1. Turn alerts on and volume up

Keep Wireless Emergency Alerts enabled and make sure your phone will sound loudly overnight.

2. Add a backup alert method

Use a weather radio or trusted weather app in case one alert channel fails.

3. Keep key items by the bed

Place shoes, flashlight, keys, and glasses where you can grab them in seconds.

4. Clear your safe place path

Make sure hallways and doorways are clear so everyone can move quickly in the dark.

5. Review the household plan

Confirm where adults, children, and pets will go if a warning is issued overnight.

6. Charge devices now

Charge phones and battery packs before sleeping so updates stay available during outages.

If Alerts Are Active Tonight

If you see a Watch

Your area could have dangerous weather later tonight. Keep your plan ready and stay close to updates.

If you see a Warning

Take action right away. Move to your safe place immediately and stay there until danger passes.

Bedside Grab List

  • Flashlight with fresh batteries
  • Charged phone
  • Shoes and weather-ready clothing
  • Keys, wallet, and medications
  • Pet leash or carrier

Family Wake-Up Plan

Pick one clear sentence everyone recognizes, such as “Warning now, safe place now.” Keep it short and direct.

Practice one quick nighttime drill so everyone knows the route and meeting spot without confusion.
